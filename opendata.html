How We Are Doing

This graph provides an overview of how we are doing at the centre. It shows the number of clients and the allocation of resources.

Number of Clients

Shows the number of clients that were referred to the night shelter by week. While this isn't indicative of the number of homeless people around Gwent/Newport, it does indicate the number of people being assigned interim accommodation through the local authorities. [Dummy Data].


Resource Allocation

Shows how we allocate resources. Very little is spent on management or administration, as we're not running a large organisation with a corporate structure. In fact, the night shelter spends most the finances on maintaining the property. With the other night shelter, the vast majority is spend on consumables. [Dummy Data].


Housing (Wales) Act 2014 Outcomes

These are rough and informal statistics regarding the 2015/2016 outcomes of the Housing (Wales) Act 2014. Most applications for assessment resulted in the prevention of homelessness. In the vast majority of cases where this outcome wasn't achieved, accommodation was provided. The failure rate is around 3.21%.
